Chef Game Application

Java

This is a Java game application based on MVC and other design patterns like Observer, Decorator, Iterator, and Factory.

Middle Panel

"Intro" button: show some introduction of the game

Initially, each position is gray which means there is no plate on it.

When we click "rotate" button, there should be random colorful plates placed on the belt.

Random means: (User can create customized sushi on the right panel)

1. random plate(blue, yellow, gold, red) Each kind of plate has specific price. The price of gold plate is changeable.
2. random ingredient
3. random position
4. random sushi type
5. random chef type and chef name: chef can create Sashimi, Nigiri, or Roll.
6. random customer: customers would consume sushi on the plates randomly. (No cutomer GUI in this project)

When we click on each plate, there should be the information of the plate

The alert window includes the following information

1. The type of the sushi: Sashimi, Nigiri, or Roll
2. If the sushi is roll, the ingredients it has
3. The name of the chef who created this sushi
4. The age of the plate: the rotation times of the plate

Left Panel

"Balance" button: show the balance of each chef from highest to lowest.

Balance = 100(initial blance) + price of consumed plates - cost of ingredients(create sushi will cost some money of chef)

"Spoiled Amount" button: show the spoiled amount of sushi from lowest to highest

Sushi could be spoiled after several rotations

"Consumed Amount" button: show the consumed amount of sushi from highest to lowest

Random customers will consume sushi created from different chef

Right Panel

3 types of sushi in total: Sashimi, Nigiri, and Roll

1. Sashimi and Nigiri could have only one type of ingredient from five choices
2. Roll could have up to 8 types of ingredients from 8 choices. Each ingredient can be added up to 1.5 oz
3. Player can only create one type of sushi once and must choose the plate type and position.

Nigiri and Sashimi Making

If the user choose one kind of sushi (Sashimi, Nigiri, or ROll), the other two are set disabled.

Roll Making

To create roll, the player should customize the types and amount of the ingredients

Clear Choice

The player could clear the choice and recreate the sushi he/she wants

Gold Plate Price

If the player choose the gold plate, a price chooser appears. As a chef, he/she should cutomize the price of the plate

Google Home Usability Test and Evaluation

Usability Test and Evaluation.

Task List

Three interaction tasks that incorporated executing commands, checking information, and carrying a casual conversation. Participants might want to read the task script prior to the experiment and let the moderator know when they completed each task:

  • Issue news and music playing commands:

The participant is required to request a piece of news of any topics at that day and then play a music he or she likes. Participants’ queries were expected to be as natural as in their real life. Completion: the participant gets news they like and a song by his or her issued artist or genre Partial completion: the participant gets unrelated news, or get an undesired music as the response.

  • Check information about attractions in one city and flight information:

The participant is required to learn about some interesting attractions in Oslo in Norway, Cape Town in South Africa, or Geneva in Switzerland and sequentially turn to seek flight information (date, price). Completion: the participant gets names of at least three attractions and information of the most recent flight (price and date) Partial completion: One or more than one piece of information fails to be obtained.

  • Start and carry a casual conversation:

The participant is required to initiate a conversation and keep it going on as long as he or she wanted to reach an end. Participants were provided cues of questions in task script and had no limitations in topic choices.

Findings

In terms of Google Home:

  • Overall, it is easy for users to get started with Google Home, but hard to fully complete the task.
  • GH falls short of user’s expectation when dealing with multi-step information checking tasks.
  • GH underperforms the imperative requests than interrogative requests.
  • The design of dialogue register (Hey Google) and feedback visualization (LED light) receives an insufficiently satisfactory assessment
In terms of users:
  • The user experience has some effects on Google Home’s performance, especially in multi-step tasks.
  • Miscommunication can be a critical factor that impacts the user’s satisfaction.
  • The miscommunication may be associated with length and type of user’s requests.
  • While issuing request, users prefer interrogative requests to imperative requests.
  • The attitudes and expectations towards GH varies across individuals. Users may adjust their expectation along performing the task, which correspondingly lead to certain responses or actions.
